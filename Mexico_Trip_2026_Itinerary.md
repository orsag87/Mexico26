## Day 4 — Hot Air Balloon + Teotihuacán + The 40th Birthday

**Saturday, October 31 — Halloween / Día de los Muertos Eve**

### 4:30 AM — Yes, 4:30 AM — HOT AIR BALLOON OVER THE PYRAMIDS

Hotel pickup ~4:30am. Coffee and pastries at the balloon port as the sky turns pink. At sunrise, float 200+ metres above the ancient pyramids of Teotihuacán—the Pyramid of the Sun, Pyramid of the Moon, and the Avenue of the Dead stretching below. 40–50 min flight. Champagne toast when you land.

**Book with:** [Volare](https://volare.com.mx/en/home/) (est. 2005, most trusted) or [Flying Pictures Mexico](https://flyingpictures.mx/en/) (British company, FAA-certified pilots). ~$165–180 USD/pp shared flight. Includes breakfast + champagne.

📍 [Balloon launch site on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Teotihuacan+Hot+Air+Balloon)

### Morning — Explore Teotihuacán on Foot

After landing, walk the ruins. Climb the [Pyramid of the Sun](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pyramid+of+the+Sun+Teotihuacan) (3rd largest pyramid on Earth, 248 steps). Walk the Avenue of the Dead. Admire the Pyramid of the Moon. Allow 2–2.5 hours. Entry ~$5 USD. Back to CDMX by early afternoon.

📍 [Teotihuacán on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Teotihuacan+Pyramids+Mexico)

> **Fun Fact:** Teotihuacán means "City of the Gods" in Nahuatl—but nobody knows who actually built it. The Aztecs arrived 700 years after the city was abandoned and believed only gods could have created something so massive. The Pyramid of the Sun contains more volume than the Great Pyramid of Giza.

### Afternoon — Rest Up or Optional: El Bazar Sabado

You've been up since 4:30am—NAP. Or, if you're wired: [El Bazar Sabado](https://www.elbazaarsabado.mx/) (San Ángel, Saturdays only, 10am–5pm). Curated artisan market in a colonial courtyard since 1960. Ceramics, textiles, jewelry, folk art. Birthday crew: coordinate the cake (ask Airbnb host for local pastelería).

### Evening — THE 40TH BIRTHDAY

**Dinner:** [Máximo Bistro](https://www.maximobistrot.com.mx/) or [Rosetta](https://rosetta.com.mx/en). Tell them it's a 40th when you book. Splurge: [Pujol](https://pujol.com.mx/) (world-ranked, book months ahead, ~$120–150/pp).

**The vibe:** It's Halloween AND Día de los Muertos Eve. The whole city is in skeleton face paint and costumes. Get painted at a street artist and hit the bars as beautiful skeletons. Birthday party meets once-in-a-lifetime cultural moment.

**After dinner:** [Baltra Bar](https://baltra.bar/) → Roma/Condesa Saturday night bar crawl → wherever the music takes you.

**Cost:** ~$280–350/person (balloon + food + birthday dinner)

## Day 5 — Muertos Parade + Mixquic Cemetery Vigil

**Sunday, November 1 — Día de los Muertos**

### Morning — Brunch + Ofrenda Wandering

Brunch at [Lardo](https://www.lardo.com.mx/) (Condesa) or [Niddo](https://niddo.menu-world.com/). Walk your neighbourhood—every home, restaurant, and shop has elaborate ofrendas (altars for the dead, decorated with marigolds, photos, favourite foods). Parque México will have pop-up installations. Take your time; each tells a personal story.

### Afternoon 2:00 PM — THE 2026 DÍA DE LOS MUERTOS PARADE

Confirmed for Sunday, November 1, 2026. Starts at **Puerta de Leones** (Chapultepec) ~2pm, proceeds down **Paseo de la Reforma**, ends at the [Zócalo](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/plaza-de-la-constitucion-the-zocalo/?lang=en). 1,000+ performers, giant skull marionettes, alebrije floats, brass bands. Get to Reforma 1 hour early.

📍 [Parade route on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Paseo+de+la+Reforma+Mexico+City)

> **Fun Fact:** The CDMX Muertos parade only started in 2016—inspired by the opening scene of the James Bond film Spectre (2015). What began as fiction became one of the world's most spectacular cultural celebrations, now drawing 2 million+ spectators annually.

### Night — THE CEMETERY VIGIL — La Alumbrada at Mixquic

[San Andrés Mixquic](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/san-andres-mixquic/)—"Barrio Mágico," ~1.5 hours south of Centro. At 8pm, church bells ring, cemetery lights dim, and thousands of candles illuminate every grave. Families decorate with marigolds, copal incense drifts through the air.

Book: [Viator Mixquic Tour](https://www.viator.com/tours/Mexico-City/Day-of-the-Dead-in-Mixquic-Special-event-1-and-2-November/d628-168021P14/) or private driver (~$30–40 each way). Leave by 6–7pm.

📍 [Mixquic on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=San+Andres+Mixquic+Cemetery)

> **Respect is everything.** This is a living spiritual tradition. Families are welcoming to respectful visitors. Ask before photographing. Be present. Be quiet.

> **Fun Fact:** The cempasúchil (marigold) is the flower of the dead—its vivid orange colour and intense scent are believed to guide spirits back from the afterlife. At Mixquic, the entire cemetery glows orange and gold.

**Cost:** ~$60–90/person

## Day 6 — Xochimilco + Bonus CDMX Day

**Monday, November 2**

### Morning — Slow Start + Brunch

After the intensity of Muertos: sleep in. Coffee at [Odette](https://odette.com.mx/) or [Panadería Rosetta](https://elenareygadas.com/en/). Zócalo altars and installations still on display if you missed anything.

### Afternoon — XOCHIMILCO — Floating Gardens

[Xochimilco](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Embarcadero+Nuevo+Nativitas+Xochimilco)—the last remnants of the Aztec canal system. Board a colourful trajinera (gondola), bring beer and mezcal, float through ancient canals while mariachi boats pull alongside to serenade you and food vendors sell elote, quesadillas, and micheladas from their own boats. 2–3 hours. Hire a full trajinera (~$25–30 USD/hour, fits the whole group).

📍 [Xochimilco on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Xochimilco+Floating+Gardens+Mexico+City)

> **Fun Fact:** Xochimilco's canals are all that remain of Lake Texcoco, where the Aztec capital Tenochtitlán was built on artificial islands called chinampas. These floating gardens still produce food for CDMX today—a 700-year-old agricultural system that's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

### Evening — Last CDMX Night

**Dinner:** Anywhere you missed—[Choza](https://www.instagram.com/chozamx/) (Thai-Mexican), [Cariñito](https://guide.michelin.com/us/en/ciudad-de-mexico/cuauhtemoc_1995126/restaurant/carinito-tacos), or street tacos from your favourite taquero. **Nightcap:** [Cananea](https://www.instagram.com/cananeamx/) or one last mezcal at [Baltra](https://baltra.bar/).

**Cost:** ~$50–80/person

## Day 10 — Monte Albán + Last Oaxaca Night

**Thursday, November 5**

### Morning — Monte Albán

[Monte Albán](https://inah.gob.mx/zonas/94-zona-arqueologica-de-monte-alban)—ancient Zapotec capital, UNESCO World Heritage, mountaintop ruins with 360° valley views. 30 min from Centro. Opens 10am. ~$5 USD. Allow 2–2.5 hours.

**Adventure alternative:** [Hierve el Agua](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hierve+el+Agua+Oaxaca)—petrified waterfalls with natural infinity pools overlooking the valley. 1.5 hours from Oaxaca. Absolutely unreal. Combine with a mezcal palenque (distillery) stop on the way back.

📍 [Monte Albán on map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Monte+Alban+Oaxaca)

> **Fun Fact:** Monte Albán was built by the Zapotec civilization around 500 BCE on a levelled mountaintop. At its peak it housed 25,000 people. The astronomical alignments are still accurate 2,500 years later.

## Day 13 — Beach Day + Bioluminescence

**Sunday, November 8**

### Morning / Afternoon — Full Beach Mode

Sleep in. [Carrizalillo](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Playa+Carrizalillo+Puerto+Escondido) or [Sativa](https://sativapuerto.com/) beach lunch on Zicatela. Hammock. Ceviche. Don't move until sunset.

### Evening — BIOLUMINESCENCE TOUR

Depart ~7pm. 30-min drive to [Laguna Manialtepec](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Laguna+Manialtepec+Puerto+Escondido). Board a boat or SUP into the dark lagoon. Every movement in the water creates electric blue-green light—millions of bioluminescent plankton reacting to your touch. Swim in liquid starlight. ~3.5 hours total.

**Book with:** [Robert's Ecotours](https://robertsecotours.com/tours/bioluminescent-lagoon/) or [Vidasurf Ecotours](https://www.vidasurfecotours.com/en/bioluminescence/) (includes SUP/kayak + boat transport). ~$50–80/pp.

> **Fun Fact:** Manialtepec's bioluminescence is caused by dinoflagellates—microscopic organisms that produce light when disturbed. Swimming at night, every stroke paints a trail of blue fire through the water. It's like being inside a galaxy.

**Cost:** ~$80–120/person

# The Logistics Guide

## Flights

Book an **open-jaw ticket**: YVR → MEX (Oct 28), then MEX → YVR (Nov 11). Check [Google Flights](https://www.google.com/travel/flights), [Aeromexico](https://www.aeromexico.com/), [WestJet](https://www.westjet.com/).

Internal flights: [Volaris](https://www.volaris.com/) and [VivaAerobus](https://www.vivaaerobus.com/)—book early for $40–80/leg.

## Money

- **Pesos are king.** Use ATMs inside bank branches (Santander, HSBC, Banorte). Decline the "conversion"—always be charged in pesos.
- Visa/Mastercard widely accepted in CDMX and Oaxaca. Puerto Escondido is more cash-heavy.
- Consider a [Wise](https://wise.com/) travel card for minimal fees.

## Tipping

- Restaurants: 15–20% (check if "servicio" is added)
- Bars: 10–15% or round up
- Hotels: 50–100 pesos/night housekeeping
- Tour guides: 100–200 pesos/person

## Safety

- All three cities are safe for tourists with normal awareness
- Use **Uber/DiDi** over street taxis, especially at night in CDMX
- Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Juárez = safe night walking. Centro gets quieter late.
- **Travel insurance:** GET IT. [World Nomads](https://www.worldnomads.com/) or [Allianz](https://www.allianztravelinsurance.com/). ~$50–100 CAD for 15 days.

## Communication

- **eSIM:** [Airalo](https://www.airalo.com/) or [Holafly](https://esim.holafly.com/)—~$20–30 USD for 15 days. Set up at YVR.
- **WhatsApp** is everything in Mexico. Create the crew group chat.

## Water & Health

- Don't drink tap water. Restaurant water and ice are purified—you're fine.
- Street food is safe if the stall is busy (high turnover = fresh).
- Pharmacies everywhere: **Farmacias del Ahorro** and **Similares**.
- Bring **Dramamine** for the Oaxaca→PE mountain road and **reef-safe sunscreen**.

## Packing

- **CDMX/Oaxaca:** Layers (15–25°C). Light jacket for evenings.
- **PE:** Hot + humid (28–32°C). Shorts, tanks, swimsuits, sandals.
- One "going out" outfit per person (Almoraduz/Criollo aren't flip-flop spots)
- Comfortable walking shoes for cobblestones
- Insect repellent for PE evenings
