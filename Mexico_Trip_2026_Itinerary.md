## Pre-Trip: Mandatory Watch List

Watch these before you go. You'll understand everything differently once you're there.

- **Coco** (2017) — Pixar's love letter to Día de los Muertos. You will cry. You will understand the marigolds at Mixquic. Highest-grossing film in Mexican history.
- **Roma** (2018) — Alfonso Cuarón's masterpiece set in 1970s CDMX. Shot in the Roma neighbourhood you'll be staying in. Oscar winner.
- **Y Tu Mamá También** (2001) — Road trip through Oaxaca to a hidden beach. Captures Mexico's beauty and contradictions like nothing else. Gael García Bernal.
- **Amores Perros** (2000) — Three stories connected by a car crash in CDMX. Gritty, beautiful, unforgettable. Iñárritu's debut.
- **Frida** (2002) — Salma Hayek IS Frida Kahlo. Much filmed at Casa Azul—the museum you'll visit on Day 2.
- **Nacho Libre** (2006) — Filmed entirely in Oaxaca. Jack Black becomes a luchador. Watch this before Arena México. "Get that corn outta my face!"
- **Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown** S3E5 & S9E1 — Mexico City + Oaxaca episodes. Every Bourdain-approved spot labelled throughout this itinerary with a 🔪.

# 01 — Mexico City

**Oct 28 – Nov 3 · 6 nights · Roma Norte / Condesa**

### 🌿 420 Info — Mexico City

Mexico's Supreme Court declared personal cannabis use a constitutional right in 2021. Possession under 28g is decriminalized. Commercial sales remain in a legal grey zone, but CDMX has tolerated "Puntos 420"—open-air consumption spots. The most well-known is at **La Estela de Luz** on Paseo de la Reforma near the entrance to Chapultepec Park, where vendors sell flower, pre-rolls, edibles, and concentrates. Prices: $10–100 MXN ($0.50–5 USD). Be chill, be discreet in public, don't carry large amounts, and never cross international borders with anything.

## Day 4 — Hot Air Balloon + Teotihuacán + Kat's 40th

**Saturday, October 31 — Halloween / Día de los Muertos Eve**

### 4:30 AM — Yes, 4:30 AM — HOT AIR BALLOON OVER THE PYRAMIDS

Hotel pickup ~4:30am. Coffee and pastries at the balloon port as the sky turns pink. At sunrise, float 200+ metres above the ancient pyramids of Teotihuacán—the Pyramid of the Sun, Pyramid of the Moon, and the Avenue of the Dead stretching below. 40–50 min flight. Champagne toast when you land.

**Book with:** [Volare](https://volare.com.mx/en/home/) (est. 2005, most trusted) or [Flying Pictures Mexico](https://flyingpictures.mx/en/) (British company, FAA-certified pilots). ~$165–180 USD/pp shared flight. Includes breakfast + champagne.

> 🙋 **Scared of heights?** No stress. Skip the balloon and meet everyone at the pyramids. Book an **early morning guided walking tour** instead—an archaeologist takes you through the site as the gates open, before the crowds. Some tours include an obsidian workshop visit + mezcal and pulque tasting on the way back. Check [Viator guided tours](https://www.viator.com/Mexico-City-tours/Historical-Guided-Walks/d628-g1-c53) (~$39 USD). Same pyramids, both feet on the ground, and you still get the sunrise light.

### Morning — Explore Teotihuacán on Foot

After landing, walk the ruins. Walk the Avenue of the Dead. Admire the Pyramid of the Sun (3rd largest pyramid on Earth) and the Pyramid of the Moon. Allow 2–2.5 hours. Entry ~$5 USD. Back to CDMX by early afternoon.

> **Fun Fact:** Teotihuacán means "City of the Gods" in Nahuatl—but nobody knows who actually built it. The Aztecs arrived 700 years after the city was abandoned and believed only gods could have created something so massive.

### Afternoon — Rest Up or Optional: El Bazar Sabado

You've been up since 4:30am—NAP. Or, if you're wired: [El Bazar Sabado](https://www.elbazaarsabado.mx/) (San Ángel, Saturdays only, 10am–5pm). Curated artisan market in a colonial courtyard since 1960. Birthday crew: coordinate the cake for Kat.

### Evening — KAT'S 40TH BIRTHDAY 🎂

**Dinner:** [Máximo Bistro](https://www.maximobistrot.com.mx/) 🔪 Bourdain Pick or [Rosetta](https://rosetta.com.mx/en). Tell them it's a 40th when you book. Splurge: [Pujol](https://pujol.com.mx/) (world-ranked, book months ahead, ~$120–150/pp). Or go more casual with [Masala y Maíz](https://www.masalaymaiz.com/) for something truly unique.

**The vibe:** It's Halloween AND Día de los Muertos Eve. The whole city is in skeleton face paint and costumes. Get painted at a street artist and hit the bars as beautiful skeletons. Kat's birthday meets once-in-a-lifetime cultural moment.

### Late Night — The 40th Birthday Bar Crawl 🔥

This is the big one. Saturday night + Halloween + Día de los Muertos Eve + Kat's 40th. The whole city is out. Here's the move:

- **Start:** [Licorería Limantour](https://limfruits.com/) (Roma) — World's #7 bar. Two perfect cocktails each. The one to open with.

## Day 5 — Muertos Parade + Mixquic Cemetery Vigil

**Sunday, November 1 — Día de los Muertos**

### Morning — Brunch + Ofrenda Wandering

Brunch at [Lardo](https://www.lardo.com.mx/) (Condesa) or [Niddo](https://niddo.menu-world.com/). Walk your neighbourhood—every home, restaurant, and shop has elaborate ofrendas (altars for the dead, decorated with marigolds, photos, favourite foods). Parque México will have pop-up installations.

### Afternoon 2:00 PM — THE 2026 DÍA DE LOS MUERTOS PARADE

Confirmed for Sunday, November 1, 2026. Starts at **Puerta de Leones** (Chapultepec) ~2pm, proceeds down **Paseo de la Reforma**, ends at the [Zócalo](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/plaza-de-la-constitucion-the-zocalo/?lang=en). 1,000+ performers, giant skull marionettes, alebrije floats, brass bands. Get to Reforma 1 hour early.

> **Fun Fact:** The CDMX Muertos parade only started in 2016—inspired by the opening scene of the James Bond film Spectre (2015). What began as fiction became one of the world's most spectacular cultural celebrations, now drawing 2 million+ spectators annually.

### Night — THE CEMETERY VIGIL — La Alumbrada at Mixquic

[San Andrés Mixquic](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/san-andres-mixquic/)—"Barrio Mágico," ~1.5 hours south of Centro. At 8pm, church bells ring, cemetery lights dim, and thousands of candles illuminate every grave.

Book: [Viator Mixquic Tour](https://www.viator.com/tours/Mexico-City/Day-of-the-Dead-in-Mixquic-Special-event-1-and-2-November/d628-168021P14/) or private driver (~$30–40 each way). Leave by 6–7pm.

> **Respect is everything.** This is a living spiritual tradition. Ask before photographing. Be present. Be quiet.

**Cost:** ~$60–90/person

## Day 6 — Xochimilco + Bonus CDMX Day

**Monday, November 2**

### Morning — Slow Start + Bourdain Breakfast

After the intensity of Muertos: sleep in. Coffee at [Odette](https://odette.com.mx/) or [Panadería Rosetta](https://elenareygadas.com/en/). For the adventurous: seek out **Fonda Margarita** 🔪 Bourdain Pick—working-class breakfast spot where stews have been simmering since 1am over charcoal.

### Afternoon — XOCHIMILCO — Floating Gardens

[Xochimilco](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Embarcadero+Nuevo+Nativitas+Xochimilco)—the last remnants of the Aztec canal system. Board a colourful trajinera (gondola), bring beer and mezcal, float through ancient canals while mariachi boats pull alongside to serenade you. 2–3 hours. Hire a full trajinera (~$25–30 USD/hour, fits the whole group).

> **Fun Fact:** Xochimilco's canals are all that remain of Lake Texcoco, where the Aztec capital Tenochtitlán was built on artificial islands called chinampas. A 700-year-old agricultural system that's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

### Evening — Last CDMX Night

**Dinner:** Anything you missed—[Choza](https://www.instagram.com/chozamx/) (Thai-Mexican), [Cariñito](https://guide.michelin.com/us/en/ciudad-de-mexico/cuauhtemoc_1995126/restaurant/carinito-tacos), [Masala y Maíz](https://www.masalaymaiz.com/), or street tacos. ## Day 10 — Monte Albán + Last Oaxaca Night

### 🌿 420 Info — Puerto Escondido

PE is very chill about cannabis. The surf/bohemian culture means it's widely tolerated, especially in La Punta. Same rules as CDMX: keep it under 28g, be discreet, don't be obnoxious about it. Enjoy responsibly on the villa terrace with that ocean view.

## 🎯 PE Activities Menu

Mix and match throughout your stay — over-suggest, pick what vibes:

- **Mezcal & Tequila** — Visit a sixth-generation mezcal distillery in the hills outside PE. Walk through the production process, taste 10+ different mezcals. Book through [EcoAdventures PE](https://ecoadventurespuertoescondido.com/) or your villa concierge.

- **Scooter Coast Ride** — Rent scooters and ride west to Mazunte (hippie beach town, sea turtle sanctuary) and San Agustinillo (tiny cove, great snorkelling). ~1 hour ride through coastal mountains.
- **Shopping & Culture** — PE's La Punta and Zicatela neighbourhoods are full of independent boutiques, surf shops, and small galleries. Vintage finds, handmade jewelry, local art, and surf culture everywhere.
- **Dolphin & Whale Watching** — Morning boat tour: pods of 1,000+ dolphins spotted regularly. ~$50–90/pp via [Puerto Boat Tours](https://puerto-boat-tour.com.mx/). November = early humpback whale season.
- **Turtle Release** — [Playa Bacocho](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Playa+Bacocho+Puerto+Escondido) at sunset—baby sea turtles released into the ocean. November is peak nesting season.

## Day 13 — Beach Day + Bioluminescence

### Morning / Afternoon — Full Beach Mode

### Evening — BIOLUMINESCENCE TOUR

Depart ~7pm. 30-min drive to [Laguna Manialtepec](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Laguna+Manialtepec+Puerto+Escondido). Board a boat or SUP into the dark lagoon. Every movement in the water creates electric blue-green light—millions of bioluminescent plankton reacting to your touch. Swim in liquid starlight. ~3.5 hours total.

**Book with:** [Robert's Ecotours](https://robertsecotours.com/tours/bioluminescent-lagoon/) or [Vidasurf Ecotours](https://www.vidasurfecotours.com/en/bioluminescence/) (includes SUP/kayak + boat transport). ~$50–80/pp.

> **Fun Fact:** Manialtepec's bioluminescence is caused by dinoflagellates—microscopic organisms that produce light when disturbed. Swimming at night, every stroke paints a trail of blue fire through the water. It's like being inside a galaxy.

**Cost:** ~$80–120/person

# The Logistics Guide

## Flights

Book an **open-jaw ticket**: YVR → MEX (Oct 28), then MEX → YVR (Nov 11). Check [Google Flights](https://www.google.com/travel/flights), [Aeromexico](https://www.aeromexico.com/), [WestJet](https://www.westjet.com/).

Internal flights: [Volaris](https://www.volaris.com/) and [VivaAerobus](https://www.vivaaerobus.com/)—book early for $40–80/leg.

## Money

- **Pesos are king.** Use ATMs inside bank branches (Santander, HSBC, Banorte). Decline the "conversion"—always be charged in pesos.
- Visa/Mastercard widely accepted in CDMX and Oaxaca. Puerto Escondido is more cash-heavy.
- Consider a [Wise](https://wise.com/) travel card for minimal fees.

## Tipping

- Restaurants: 15–20% (check if "servicio" is added)
- Bars: 10–15% or round up
- Hotels: 50–100 pesos/night housekeeping
- Tour guides: 100–200 pesos/person

## Safety

- All three cities are safe for tourists with normal awareness
- Use **Uber/DiDi** over street taxis, especially at night in CDMX
- Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Juárez = safe night walking. Centro gets quieter late.
- **Travel insurance:** GET IT. [World Nomads](https://www.worldnomads.com/) or [Allianz](https://www.allianztravelinsurance.com/). ~$50–100 CAD for 15 days.

## 420 Ground Rules

- Personal possession under 28g decriminalized since 2021 (Supreme Court ruling)
- Grey market—no licensed dispensaries yet. CDMX has tolerated "Puntos 420" zones
- Chill at the villa, be discreet in public, never carry large amounts
- **NEVER** cross international borders with anything. Not even edibles. Not even residue.
- PE is very relaxed but still technically grey. Villa terrace = perfect spot

## Scooters & Getting Around

- **CDMX:** Uber/DiDi everywhere. Metro is excellent but crowded. No scooter needed.
- **Oaxaca:** Walkable. Taxis cheap. Uber available.
- **PE:** Scooter territory. Rent from [Rent Scooters PE](https://rentscootersinpuertoescondido.com/), [Robert's Ecotours](https://robertsecotours.com/tours/rental-scooter-pacific-puerto-escondido/), or [Brand X Moto](https://brandxmototours.com/). Valid driver's licence required. Helmets mandatory.

## Communication

- **eSIM:** [Airalo](https://www.airalo.com/) or [Holafly](https://esim.holafly.com/)—~$20–30 USD for 15 days. Set up at YVR.
- **WhatsApp** is everything in Mexico. Create the crew group chat. Nattie, Honzi, Kat, Tashaun, Casey—everyone in.

## Water & Health

- Don't drink tap water. Restaurant water and ice are purified—you're fine.
- Street food is safe if the stall is busy (high turnover = fresh).
- Pharmacies everywhere: **Farmacias del Ahorro** and **Similares**.
- Bring **Dramamine** for the Oaxaca→PE mountain road and **reef-safe sunscreen**.

## Packing

- **CDMX/Oaxaca:** Layers (15–25°C). Light jacket for evenings.
- **PE:** Hot + humid (28–32°C). Shorts, tanks, swimsuits, sandals.
- One "going out" outfit per person (Almoraduz/Criollo aren't flip-flop spots)
- Comfortable walking shoes for cobblestones
- Insect repellent for PE evenings
