420 Info — Mexico City

Mexico’s Supreme Court declared personal cannabis use a constitutional right in 2021. Possession under 28g is decriminalized. Commercial sales remain in a legal grey zone, but CDMX has tolerated “Puntos 420”—open-air consumption spots. The most well-known is at La Estela de Luz on Paseo de la Reforma near the entrance to Chapultepec Park, where vendors sell flower, pre-rolls, edibles, and concentrates. Prices: $10–100 MXN ($0.50–5 USD). Be chill, be discreet in public, don’t carry large amounts, and never cross international borders with anything.

HOT AIR BALLOON OVER THE PYRAMIDS

Hotel pickup ~4:30am. Coffee and pastries at the balloon port as the sky turns pink. At sunrise, float 200+ metres above the ancient pyramids of Teotihuacán—the Pyramid of the Sun, Pyramid of the Moon, and the Avenue of the Dead stretching below. 40–50 min flight. Champagne toast when you land.

Book with: Volare (est. 2005, most trusted) or Flying Pictures Mexico (British company, FAA-certified pilots). ~$165–180 USD/pp shared flight. Includes breakfast + champagne.

THE 2026 DÍA DE LOS MUERTOS PARADE

Confirmed for Sunday, November 1, 2026. Starts at Puerta de Leones (Chapultepec) ~2pm, proceeds down Paseo de la Reforma, ends at the Zócalo. 1,000+ performers, giant skull marionettes, alebrije floats, brass bands. Get to Reforma 1 hour early.

📍 Parade route on map
The CDMX Muertos parade only started in 2016—inspired by the opening scene of the James Bond film Spectre (2015). What began as fiction became one of the world’s most spectacular cultural celebrations, now drawing 2 million+ spectators annually.
Night — THE CEMETERY VIGIL

La Alumbrada at Mixquic

San Andrés Mixquic—“Barrio Mágico,” ~1.5 hours south of Centro. At 8pm, church bells ring, cemetery lights dim, and thousands of candles illuminate every grave. Families decorate with marigolds, copal incense drifts through the air.

Book: Viator Mixquic Tour or private driver (~$30–40 each way). Leave by 6–7pm.

📍 Mixquic on map
Respect is everything. This is a living spiritual tradition. Families are welcoming to respectful visitors. Ask before photographing. Be present. Be quiet.
The cempasúchil (marigold) is the flower of the dead—its vivid orange colour and intense scent are believed to guide spirits back from the afterlife. At Mixquic, the entire cemetery glows orange and gold.
~$60–90/person

Afternoon — XOCHIMILCO

Floating Gardens

Xochimilco—the last remnants of the Aztec canal system. Board a colourful trajinera (gondola), bring beer and mezcal, float through ancient canals while mariachi boats pull alongside to serenade you and food vendors sell elote, quesadillas, and micheladas from their own boats. 2–3 hours. Hire a full trajinera (~$25–30 USD/hour, fits the whole group).

📍 Xochimilco on map
Xochimilco’s canals are all that remain of Lake Texcoco, where the Aztec capital Tenochtitlán was built on artificial islands called chinampas. These floating gardens still produce food for CDMX today—a 700-year-old agricultural system that’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

420 Info — Puerto Escondido

PE is very chill about cannabis. The surf/bohemian culture means it’s widely tolerated, especially in La Punta. Same rules as CDMX: keep it under 28g, be discreet, don’t be obnoxious about it. Enjoy responsibly on the villa terrace with that ocean view.

Puerto Escondido means “Hidden Port”—it was a secret smuggling cove until the 1960s when surfers discovered Playa Zicatela, now called the “Mexican Pipeline” and home to one of the most powerful beach breaks on Earth. November is the start of high season—perfect timing.

🎯 PE Activities Menu

Mix and match throughout your stay — over-suggest, pick what vibes

Mezcal & Tequila

Visit a sixth-generation mezcal distillery in the hills outside PE. Walk through the production process, taste 10+ different mezcals. Some tours combine mezcal with salsa dancing. Book through EcoAdventures PE or your villa concierge.

Markets & Food

Mercado Benito Juárez—the main market. Fresh seafood, produce, prepared food stalls, and local artisan goods. Go hungry. Also check out the smaller neighbourhood markets for the real local experience.

Scooter Coast Ride

Rent scooters and ride west to Mazunte (hippie beach town, sea turtle sanctuary) and San Agustinillo (tiny cove, great snorkelling). ~1 hour ride through coastal mountains. Stop at roadside palapa restaurants for fresh fish and cold beer. Make a day of it.

Shopping & Culture

PE’s La Punta and Zicatela neighbourhoods are full of independent boutiques, surf shops, and small galleries. Explore the bohemian streets—vintage finds, handmade jewelry, local art, and surf culture everywhere. Ask around for pop-up markets and art openings.

Dolphin & Whale Watching

Morning boat tour: pods of 1,000+ dolphins spotted regularly. ~$50–90/pp via Puerto Boat Tours. November = early humpback whale season. Keep your eyes peeled.

Turtle Release

Playa Bacocho at sunset—baby sea turtles released into the ocean. November is peak nesting season. Check locally for times.

Evening — BIOLUMINESCENCE TOUR

Manialtepec Lagoon Glows

Depart ~7pm. 30-min drive to Laguna Manialtepec. Board a boat or SUP into the dark lagoon. Every movement in the water creates electric blue-green light—millions of bioluminescent plankton reacting to your touch. Swim in liquid starlight. ~3.5 hours total.

Book with: Robert’s Ecotours or Vidasurf Ecotours (includes SUP/kayak + boat transport). ~$50–80/pp.

Manialtepec’s bioluminescence is caused by dinoflagellates—microscopic organisms that produce light when disturbed. Swimming at night, every stroke paints a trail of blue fire through the water. It’s like being inside a galaxy.
~$80–120/person

The Logistics Guide

Flights

Book an open-jaw ticket: YVR → MEX (Oct 28), then MEX → YVR (Nov 11). Check Google Flights, Aeromexico, WestJet.

Internal flights: Volaris and VivaAerobus—book early for $40–80/leg.

Money

  • Pesos are king. Use ATMs inside bank branches (Santander, HSBC, Banorte). Decline the “conversion”—always be charged in pesos.
  • Visa/Mastercard widely accepted in CDMX and Oaxaca. Puerto Escondido is more cash-heavy.
  • Consider a Wise travel card for minimal fees.

Tipping

  • Restaurants: 15–20% (check if “servicio” is added)
  • Bars: 10–15% or round up
  • Hotels: 50–100 pesos/night housekeeping
  • Tour guides: 100–200 pesos/person

Safety

  • All three cities are safe for tourists with normal awareness
  • Use Uber/DiDi over street taxis, especially at night in CDMX
  • Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Juárez = safe night walking. Centro gets quieter late.
  • Travel insurance: GET IT. World Nomads or Allianz. ~$50–100 CAD for 15 days.

420 Ground Rules

  • Personal possession under 28g decriminalized since 2021 (Supreme Court ruling)
  • Grey market—no licensed dispensaries yet. CDMX has tolerated “Puntos 420” zones
  • Chill at the villa, be discreet in public, never carry large amounts
  • NEVER cross international borders with anything. Not even edibles. Not even residue.
  • PE is very relaxed but still technically grey. Villa terrace = perfect spot

Scooters & Getting Around

  • CDMX: Uber/DiDi everywhere. Metro is excellent but crowded. No scooter needed.
  • Oaxaca: Walkable. Taxis cheap. Uber available.
  • PE: Scooter territory. Rent from Rent Scooters PE, Robert’s Ecotours, or Brand X Moto. Valid driver’s licence required. Helmets mandatory.

Communication

  • eSIM: Airalo or Holafly—~$20–30 USD for 15 days. Set up at YVR.
  • WhatsApp is everything in Mexico. Create the crew group chat. Nattie, Honzi, Kat, Tashaun, Casey—everyone in.

Water & Health

  • Don’t drink tap water. Restaurant water and ice are purified—you’re fine.
  • Street food is safe if the stall is busy (high turnover = fresh).
  • Pharmacies everywhere: Farmacias del Ahorro and Similares.
  • Bring Dramamine for the Oaxaca→PE mountain road and reef-safe sunscreen.

Packing

  • CDMX/Oaxaca: Layers (15–25°C). Light jacket for evenings.
  • PE: Hot + humid (28–32°C). Shorts, tanks, swimsuits, sandals.
  • One “going out” outfit per person (Almoraduz/Criollo aren’t flip-flop spots)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for cobblestones
  • Insect repellent for PE evenings
